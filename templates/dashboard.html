🔬 How the AI Personalisation Works

StoryBook's adaptive engine tracks five learning signals for each child and continuously adjusts story difficulty, vocabulary richness and quiz challenge to keep reading in the optimal learning zone.

📖

Vocabulary Score 0 – 10

Starts at a baseline tuned to the child's age group (3–5 → 3.0, 6–8 → 5.0, 9–12 → 6.5). Every quiz answer shifts the score: +0.3 for a correct answer, −0.2 for a wrong one. A rolling Bayesian average prevents single outliers from swinging the score too far.

Score → Vocabulary hint: <4 Introductory · 4–6.5 Grade Level · >6.5 Stretch
📚

Reading Level Kindergarten → Grade 6+

Words per minute (WPM) are measured from chapter open to the first quiz attempt. The engine compares WPM against grade-level benchmarks (Pre-K: 60, K: 80, Gr1: 100 … Gr6+: 230) to assign a reading level label, then weights that with Q&A accuracy and completion rate.

After 3 stories the engine upgrades from rule-based thresholds to a regression model for finer-grained predictions.
💡

Engagement Score 0 – 1

A composite of four signals: completion rate (did the child finish?), replay rate (did they re-read?), quiz participation (did they attempt questions?), and response time (quick answers suggest confidence). High engagement (≥0.65) unlocks richer, longer stories.

🔥 High · 📗 Medium · 📙 Low · ⚠️ At Risk — each tier adjusts the next story's length and pacing.
🎯

Story Customisation Engine

Before each story is generated, the engine computes a complexity level (Simple / Moderate / Rich) and a vocabulary hint which are injected directly into the AI prompt. This ensures the generated text is calibrated to the individual child — not a generic age group.

🌱 Simple → short sentences, familiar words · 🌿 Moderate → mixed pacing · 🌳 Rich → complex ideas, new vocabulary

📈 Reader Progress & ML Personalisation

The AI continuously learns from each child's reading behaviour — adjusting story length, vocabulary, and complexity in real time.
